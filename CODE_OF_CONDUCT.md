# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

RoundTable is committed to a respectful, inclusive, and professional collaboration environment.

Contributors, maintainers, and participants are expected to treat each other with dignity and to help maintain a harassment-free community for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of positive behavior include:

- using welcoming and inclusive language
- giving constructive, actionable feedback
- respecting differing viewpoints and technical approaches
- taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- prioritizing project and community health over individual ego

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or threats
- sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention
- personal attacks, insults, trolling, or inflammatory comments
- doxxing or sharing private information without explicit permission
- sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or collaboration channels

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- repository issues, pull requests, and discussions
- review comments and project communication channels
- community interactions where a person is representing this project

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for:

- clarifying and consistently enforcing this Code of Conduct
- taking fair, proportionate action when violations occur
- removing content or contributions that violate these standards
- protecting the privacy and safety of reporters

## Reporting a Violation

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

- report the incident privately to project maintainers
- include evidence such as links, screenshots, and timestamps when possible
- provide context that helps maintainers investigate accurately

All good-faith reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion.

## Enforcement Process

Maintainers may apply one or more of the following actions, based on severity and pattern of behavior:

1. Private warning with corrective guidance
2. Temporary restriction from discussions or contribution channels
3. Removal of content and temporary project ban
4. Permanent ban from project participation
